# Attack A Crack: 2026 Marketing Plan

*Created: March 2026*

---

## 1. Brand Persona: The Expert Jester

**Core concept:** Technical competence + radical approachability. "The contractor friend who actually knows their stuff and makes you laugh while explaining it."

### Voice Principles

1. **Lead with expertise, deliver with personality.** Every piece of content proves we know foundations. The tone makes people want to keep reading.
2. **Never dumb it down — explain it clearly.** Homeowners aren't stupid. They just haven't spent 33 years staring at cracks. Meet them where they are.
3. **Humor is the vehicle, competence is the destination.** A joke gets attention. The technical detail earns trust. Both are required.
4. **Talk like a person, not a brochure.** If it sounds like it came from a marketing department, rewrite it. If it sounds like Luc explaining something at a barbecue, ship it.

### Tone Spectrum by Content Type

| Content Type | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Emergency/urgent | Serious + reassuring | "Water in your basement? Here's what to do right now." |
| Service pages | Authoritative + approachable | "Here's exactly how we fix it — and why it works." |
| Blog posts | Educational + conversational | "Your foundation is talking. Here's how to listen." |
| Social media | Casual + personality-forward | "This crack had opinions. We had 100 PSI." |
| Location pages | Local + knowledgeable | "We know South Shore foundations like we know potholes on Route 3." |
| Case studies | Story-driven + technical | "The homeowner thought it was a cosmetic crack. It wasn't." |

### What Expert Jester Is NOT

- Not slapstick or juvenile — the humor is dry, confident, and earned
- Not dumbed-down — we explain *better*, not *less*
- Not dismissive of homeowner concerns — every crack matters to the person who found it
- Not forced — if the topic is serious (structural failure, water damage), the tone adjusts

## 2. Blog Strategy

### Publishing Cadence

- **Minimum:** 1 post per week (52 posts/year)

- **28 existing posts** mapped to calendar slots; **~35 net-new posts** needed

### Content Pillars

## 4. Paid Advertising

### Two-Funnel Approach

#### Top-Funnel (Awareness / Educational)

- **Goal:** Brand awareness, email capture, content engagement
- **Content:** Blog posts, guides, seasonal checklists
- **Targeting:** Homeowners in Zone 1-2 cities, age 30-65, interests: home improvement, home ownership
- **Platforms:** Facebook/Instagram ads, Google Display
- **Budget allocation:** 30% of paid spend

#### Bottom-Funnel (Service Intent / Conversion)

- **Goal:** Phone calls, form submissions, consultations booked
- **Content:** Service pages, "free consultation" offers, seasonal urgency
- **Targeting:** High-intent keywords (foundation crack repair near me, basement waterproofing [city])
- **Platforms:** Google Search Ads, Google Local Services Ads (LSA)
- **Budget allocation:** 70% of paid spend

### Geo-Targeting by Zone

| Zone | Investment Level | Ad Types |
|---|---|---|
| Zone 1 (Fortress) | Highest — all channels | Search + Display + Social + LSA |
| Zone 2 (Expansion) | Moderate — search + social | Search + targeted Social |
| Zone 3 (Outposts) | Minimal — organic-first | Search only (brand + high-intent) |

### Key Ad Campaigns

1. **"Free Consultation"** — Always-on, all zones. Search ads on high-intent keywords.
2. **"Spring Thaw"** — Feb-Apr, Zone 1-2. Seasonal urgency, water intrusion messaging.
3. **"Before Winter"** — Sep-Nov, Zone 1-2. Winterization, "fix it before the freeze."
4. **"Concrete Season"** — May-Sep, MA only. Driveway/patio/pool deck repair.

#### Emergency Funnel (High-Urgency Paid Search)

Distinct from top-funnel and bottom-funnel — this targets homeowners in active crisis who need help **right now**.

- **Keywords:** "water in basement now", "water in basement after rain", "emergency foundation repair [city]", "basement flooding help"
- **Landing page:** Dedicated page that strips standard navigation and focuses entirely on immediate action — "Text Us Photos" button + "Call Now" button. No blog posts, no educational content, no service explanations. These users already know they need help.
- **Ad copy:** Urgency-first. "Water in Your Basement Right Now? Text Us a Photo. Response in Minutes."
- **Budget:** Small but always-on. Emergency keywords have high CPC but extremely high conversion rates.
- **Tracking:** Separate conversion tracking from standard campaigns — these leads close faster and at higher ticket values.

#### Brand Recall via Social (Top-of-Funnel Awareness)

The SORA AI video concept (Section 3) isn't just content — it's a **brand recall play**. The goal isn't immediate conversion. It's ensuring that when a viewer's foundation cracks 6 months from now, Attack A Crack is the only company they remember.

- **Strategy:** Use surreal, goofy, or visually arresting AI-generated video content for top-of-funnel awareness on Facebook, Instagram, and TikTok
- **Why it works:** Foundation repair is a "when you need it, you need it" business. Most homeowners will never search for it proactively. But if they've laughed at 3 Attack A Crack videos in their feed, the brand is burned into memory when the crack appears.
- **Content approach:** Expert Jester personality turned up to 11. Satisfying crack-filling ASMR, "foundation horror stories" with comedic narration, absurdist "what's under your house" explainers
- **Budget:** Small — organic reach on entertaining content does the heavy lifting. Boost only the top-performing videos.
- **KPI:** Brand search volume increase (track "attack a crack" searches in GSC), not direct conversions

## 5. Email Marketing

### Campaign Types

| Campaign | Frequency | Audience | Content |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Seasonal Newsletter** | Quarterly | All subscribers | Seasonal tips, recent projects, blog highlights |
| **Spring Alert** | Annual (March) | All subscribers | Thaw damage awareness, inspection offer |
| **Fall Prep** | Annual (October) | All subscribers | Winterization checklist, pre-freeze repair urgency |
| **New Content Digest** | Monthly | Engaged subscribers | Latest blog posts, project spotlights |
| **Partner Nurture** | Monthly | Realtors, inspectors | Market updates, referral program reminders, co-marketing |
| **Post-Service Follow-up** | After service | Past customers | Warranty info, review request, referral ask |

### Automation Sequences

1. **New lead:** Consultation request → confirmation → prep guide → follow-up (if no booking)
2. **Post-repair:** Thank you → warranty details → review request (day 7) → referral ask (day 30)
3. **Seasonal trigger:** Based on weather events (heavy rain alert → water intrusion content)
4. **Realtor onboarding:** Welcome → service overview → referral process → co-branded materials

- Content performance: Which posts drove traffic/conversions?
- Zone performance: Which geo-zones are converting?

- Voice consistency: Are we maintaining Expert Jester across channels?
- Calendar adherence: Are we hitting publishing cadence?
