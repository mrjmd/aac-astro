## 1. Injection Process & Methodology

### Current Site's Four-Phase Process (Critical Gaps)

**Phase 1: Crack Preparation**
- Diamond saw blade grinding
- 1/2" wide groove, 1/4" deep
- All debris cleared

**Phase 2: Port Installation**
- **Copper ports** (explicitly preferred over plastic -- major differentiator)
- Spaced 8-10 inches apart along entire crack
- Sealed with specialized hydraulic cement

**Phase 3: Material Injection**
- **100 PSI pressure** -- "far more than the industry standard"
- Bottom-to-top application
- Complete saturation through concrete to footer level

**Phase 4: Finishing**
- Ports crimped off after injection
- Specialized surface patch applied
- "Clean rubber layer" for polished, durable finish

## 3. Bulkhead Repair Specifics

### MA vs. CT Construction Difference

"Massachusetts bulkheads often position the cold joint directly against the vertical first step rather than forming a clean horizontal seam on the floor."

### Winter Repair Advantage

Frozen ground causes moisture expansion, creating maximum joint gaps. Spring thaw compression further reinforces the repair, amplifying sealant effectiveness.

### Timeline

Most repairs completed in 2-3 hours (covered in Astro rebuild).

## 4. Crack Assessment & Structural Thresholds

- Width concern threshold: cracks exceeding 1/4 inch
- Displacement: "one side further forward than the other" -- detectable by hand
- Inspection technique: "sight down foundation walls from each corner"
- Integrity test: "prodding with a screwdriver" for flaking/crumbling
- Stair-step patterns in masonry joints = structural concern
