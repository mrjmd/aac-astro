Your home's foundation is the backbone of its structural integrity. If it's compromised, small issues can quickly turn into costly repairs. Here's how to check your foundation for problems.

## Warning Signs of Foundation Damage

### Interior Indicators

- Doors jamming or failing to latch properly
- Wall cracks, especially above doorways and windows
- Gaps forming in vinyl or ceramic tile over concrete
- Windows sticking or refusing to close completely
- Water in basement after rain — even small amounts signal foundation cracks
- White powder on basement walls (efflorescence) — mineral deposits from water passing through concrete
- A musty smell in your basement that persists year-round

### Exterior Indicators

- Walls that appear curved or bulging when sighted from corners
- Concrete showing flaking or crumbling when tested with a screwdriver
- Signs of soil pressure from glacial till or clay-heavy soil, or long-term deterioration from freeze-thaw cycles

## Foundation Crack Types: How to Classify What You See

- **Hairline cracks:** Generally safe but warrant monitoring — seal before winter to prevent freeze-thaw widening
- **Stair-step cracks:** May signal foundation movement with wall bulging, common in block/CMU foundations
- **Horizontal cracks:** Most serious; indicate lateral soil pressure from clay-heavy soil or hydrostatic pressure — may require [carbon fiber straps](/services/carbon-fiber-stitches) to prevent further bowing
- **Vertical cracks:** Common shrinkage cracks, but [professional crack injection](/services/foundation-crack-injection) ($800-$1,300 per crack) is needed if they leak or widen beyond 1/4 inch

## Structural Support and Basement Moisture Checks

- Inspect basement or crawl space posts for moist or rotting wood
- Monitor for standing water indicating drainage complications — basement floor cracks leaking water signal hydrostatic pressure
- Address moisture damage through gutter cleaning and drainage installation
- Check for a persistent musty smell in your basement, which often indicates hidden water intrusion through foundation cracks

## 10 Preventive Maintenance Tips

1. **Seal minor cracks** (we can talk you through whether DIY or professional is appropriate)
2. **Maintain consistent soil moisture** using rock beds 18"–24" from foundation
3. **Direct drainage** at least 5 feet away (sandy soil) to 10 feet (expansive soil)
4. **Eliminate standing water** through in-ground drains or gravel basins
5. **Ensure proper grading** at 3%–5% slope within 10 feet
6. **Inspect for puddles** after rainfall near the foundation perimeter
7. **Consider moisture barriers** at least 5 feet deep when grading adjustments aren't feasible
8. **Install erosion control** on steep slopes using ground covers or landscape materials
9. **Schedule yearly plumbing inspections** to catch foundation-threatening leaks
10. **Check for mold growth** in basements and crawl spaces from moisture intrusion — a musty smell in your basement often signals hidden water problems

## When NOT to DIY Foundation Repair

While monitoring hairline cracks is appropriate for homeowners, never attempt to seal an actively leaking crack with hydraulic cement or caulk — these surface treatments fail within months and can trap moisture inside the wall, accelerating freeze-thaw damage. Professional [crack injection](/services/foundation-crack-injection) fills the entire void through all 8-10 inches of concrete at 100 PSI.
