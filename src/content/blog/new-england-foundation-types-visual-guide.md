## How to Identify Your Foundation Type

_Draft: Quick identification guide — what to look for in the basement. Texture, material, construction method._

## Poured Concrete (Post-1940s)

_Draft: Most common modern type. Smooth walls, 8-10 inch thickness. Cracks are clean and injectable._

## Cinderblock / CMU (1930s-1970s)

_Draft: Stacked block construction. Stair-step crack patterns. Hollow cores complicate repair._

## Fieldstone (Pre-1900)

_Draft: Irregular stones with mortar. Common in historic New England homes. Unique repair challenges._

## Brick (1800s-1930s)

_Draft: Fired brick foundations. Mortar joint failures. Repointing and structural concerns._

## Rubble Stone (1700s-1800s)

_Draft: Oldest type in New England. Uncut stones, minimal mortar. When to stabilize vs. rebuild._
