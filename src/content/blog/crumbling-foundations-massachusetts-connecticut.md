This article addresses the crumbling foundation crisis affecting Massachusetts and Connecticut homeowners, caused by pyrrhotite contamination in concrete foundations.

## The Crumbling Foundation Crisis: A Brief History

Connecticut experienced a devastating foundation crisis between 2015 and 2018 linked to a quarry in Willington. The culprit was pyrrhotite, an iron sulfide mineral that deteriorates concrete when exposed to oxygen and water, breaking it down into sulfuric acid.

The same issue has now emerged in Massachusetts, beginning in the central region and expanding to over 40 towns, including Boston suburbs.

## Why This Problem Is So Catastrophic

Pyrrhotite-induced damage cannot be reversed. Affected foundations require complete replacement through a process of lifting the home and rebuilding the foundation, costing between $150,000 to $250,000 or more.

## Warning Signs to Watch For

Homeowners should monitor for:

- **Horizontal or web-like cracks** in concrete
- **Rust-colored stains** or white powder on surfaces
- **Visible flaking**, bowing, or crumbling
- **Damage on home exteriors** or under basement drywall
