## Foundation Sealing for Pest Control Professionals

You eliminate the pests. We seal the entry points. Foundation cracks, utility line gaps, and bulkhead joints are highways for insects and rodents—and no amount of treatment keeps them out permanently if the structural entry points stay open.

### Why Pest Control Pros Refer to Us

**We Fix the Root Cause**
Pest treatments work best when entry points are sealed. A [foundation crack injection](/services/foundation-crack-injection) fills cracks from the inside out with expanding resin, creating a permanent barrier that insects and rodents can't penetrate.

**Your Treatments Last Longer**
When the entry points are sealed, your perimeter treatments and bait stations work the way they're supposed to—without pests bypassing them through the foundation. That means fewer callbacks for you.

**Clients Get a Complete Solution**
Homeowners want the problem solved, not managed. When you recommend both pest treatment and foundation sealing, they get a permanent fix from two specialists. That's a better outcome than either service alone.

### Common Pest Entry Points We Seal

**Foundation Wall Cracks**
Even hairline cracks in poured concrete or block foundations can admit ants, termites, centipedes, and other insects. [Crack injection](/services/foundation-crack-injection) fills the full depth of the crack with expanding resin.

**Utility Line Penetrations**
Gaps around [sewer lines, well pipes, and electrical conduits](/services/sewer-well-conduit-line-repair) are major pest highways. We inject flexible urethane that seals the gap permanently without excavation.

**Bulkhead Joints**
The joint between a precast bulkhead and the poured foundation is a notorious entry point. Our [bulkhead sealing](/services/leaky-bulkhead-repair) process eliminates the gap completely.

**Floor-Wall Joints**
The cold joint where the basement floor meets the wall is a common entry point for moisture and pests. We seal it from the interior.

### Pests That Enter Through Foundations

Foundation cracks and gaps are entry points for:
- **Carpenter ants** — follow moisture through cracks
- **Termites** — exploit foundation-to-soil contact points
- **Centipedes and millipedes** — enter through damp cracks
- **Mice and rats** — gaps as small as 1/4 inch are enough
- **Spiders** — follow other insects through cracks
- **Earwigs and silverfish** — attracted to moisture around cracks
