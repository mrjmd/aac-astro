## Foundation Repair Services in Kittery, Maine

Kittery holds the distinction of being Maine's oldest town, incorporated in 1647, and its housing stock reflects centuries of New England building traditions. From the Colonial-era homes at Kittery Point to the shipyard worker housing near the Piscataqua River, the town's foundations span every era of American residential construction. These structures sit on marine clay deposits, a legacy of ancient sea levels that left behind some of the most challenging foundation soils in southern Maine.

Marine clay is a particularly difficult soil for foundations because it expands dramatically when saturated and contracts when it dries. This constant cycle of swelling and shrinking moves the soil around foundation walls, creating lateral pressure that leads to cracking and bowing. Kittery's coastal location amplifies the problem with persistent salt air that eats away at concrete and mortar, tidal influences on the water table, and the powerful Nor'easters that drive moisture through every available gap in a foundation wall.
