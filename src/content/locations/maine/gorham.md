## Foundation Repair Services in Gorham, Maine

Gorham sits just inland from the Portland metro area, a town where New England farmsteads and newer suburban developments share a landscape shaped by glacial geology. Much of Gorham is underlain by granite ledge and glacial till, the rocky soils left behind by retreating ice sheets thousands of years ago. These geological features create unique drainage patterns that funnel water laterally toward home foundations rather than allowing it to percolate downward, making foundation cracks and basement moisture persistent problems across town.

The town's inland location means it experiences the full severity of Maine's continental winter weather without the moderating influence of the coast. Frost routinely penetrates more than five feet into Gorham's soils, and the freeze-thaw cycle repeats dozens of times each season. This relentless expansion and contraction of the ground exerts enormous pressure on foundation walls. When the heavy snowpack finally melts in spring, the runoff arrives quickly and in great volume, overwhelming drainage systems and pushing against basement walls that have been stressed all winter.
