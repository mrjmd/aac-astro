## Foundation Repair Services in York, Maine

York is one of Maine's earliest settlements, and the town's architectural heritage spans from Colonial-era homes in York Village to the grand Victorian summer cottages at York Harbor and the more modest year-round housing throughout the inland neighborhoods. This range of building eras means York's foundations include everything from hand-laid fieldstone walls that predate the nation to modern poured concrete, and each type faces its own set of challenges in Maine's demanding climate.

The town's extensive coastline exposes many properties to the full force of the Atlantic. Nor'easters batter York several times each winter, driving salt-laden moisture into foundation walls and surrounding soils. The marine clay deposits that underlie much of the coastal zone expand and contract with seasonal moisture changes, creating a slow but persistent rocking motion that opens cracks in foundation walls over time. Inland neighborhoods face different but equally serious challenges, as Maine's frost line pushes past five feet and spring snowmelt sends enormous quantities of water through soils that are still partially frozen.
