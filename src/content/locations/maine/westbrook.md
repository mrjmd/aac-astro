## Foundation Repair Services in Westbrook, Maine

Westbrook grew up around the paper mills along the Presumpscot River, and the city's residential neighborhoods still reflect that industrial heritage. Rows of worker housing from the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries line the streets near Cumberland Mills and downtown, their fieldstone and rubble foundations bearing the weight of more than a century of Maine weather. The Presumpscot River, which powered those mills, continues to influence the city today by keeping water tables high and soils saturated throughout much of the year.

Maine's freeze-thaw cycle is particularly harsh on Westbrook's older foundations. Frost pushes more than five feet into the ground each winter, expanding the moisture in surrounding soils and exerting tremendous lateral pressure on basement walls. When spring arrives, the snowmelt is sudden and dramatic, sending water coursing through the ground and finding every gap in deteriorating mortar joints and cracked concrete. Homes near Duck Pond and along the river corridor face these pressures year after year.
