## Foundation Repair Services in Kennebunk, Maine

Kennebunk is one of southern Maine's most charming towns, with a village center full of Federal and Colonial-era homes that reflect its prosperous shipbuilding past. Many of these historic properties rest on fieldstone and brick foundations that were laid before the Civil War, making them among the oldest residential foundations in the region. While these structures have proven remarkably durable, nearly two centuries of Maine's extreme freeze-thaw cycles have taken their toll, and foundation cracks and moisture problems are common throughout Kennebunk's older neighborhoods.

The town's geography adds to the challenge. The Kennebunk River and Mousam River converge near the village center, and their combined influence keeps water tables high across much of the developed area. Coastal neighborhoods in the Lower Village area face Nor'easters and salt air that accelerate the breakdown of mortar joints and concrete surfaces. The deep frost line, which extends more than five feet below grade in this part of Maine, creates annual cycles of soil expansion and contraction that stress even the most solidly built foundations.
