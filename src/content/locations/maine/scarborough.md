## Foundation Repair Services in Scarborough, Maine

Scarborough stretches from the rocky shores of Higgins Beach and Pine Point inland to the farmland and newer developments around Oak Hill, giving it a diverse landscape that presents varied foundation challenges. The town's proximity to the Scarborough Marsh, one of the largest salt marshes in Maine, means that many properties contend with elevated water tables and marine clay soils that shift with seasonal moisture changes. Even homes built on higher ground face the full force of Maine's freeze-thaw cycles, where frost penetrates well beyond five feet and spring thaw arrives suddenly.

The town has seen significant residential growth over the past few decades, and many of Scarborough's newer subdivisions sit on soils that were previously agricultural land. These soils can settle unevenly beneath foundations, creating stress cracks that allow water infiltration. Meanwhile, older homes near the coast at Black Point and Pine Point face the added challenge of salt air corrosion and storm-driven moisture from Nor'easters that batter the Maine coastline several times each winter.
