## Foundation Repair Services in Andover, Massachusetts

Andover is an affluent Merrimack Valley town known for Phillips Academy, its charming village center, and miles of conservation land along the Shawsheen River. The town's housing stock spans several centuries — from fieldstone-foundation colonials near Andover Center and the historic Ballardvale village to the ranch and Colonial Revival homes built during the 1950s through 1970s suburban expansion in West Andover and Indian Ridge. This diversity of eras and architectural styles means Andover presents a wide range of foundation types and repair challenges within a single community.

The Shawsheen River system is the dominant geological influence on Andover foundations. The river and its tributaries meander through the center of town, and the associated floodplain and wetland areas maintain high groundwater levels across a significant portion of the community. Andover's soils vary from sandy glacial outwash on the higher ground of West Andover to dense, moisture-retentive clay deposits in the river valleys near Ballardvale and Shawsheen Village. Fieldstone foundations in the older neighborhoods were built directly against this soil without modern drainage or waterproofing, and after 150-plus years of moisture cycling, many have lost significant mortar and shifted under lateral earth pressure. Mid-century poured concrete foundations on the higher ground fare better but still develop stress cracks and water entry points after decades of freeze-thaw exposure.
