## Foundation Repair Services in Medford, Massachusetts

Medford is a densely built city just north of Boston where over a century of residential construction has created a rich architectural landscape of triple-deckers, Victorians, and pre-war two-families packed along tree-lined streets. The Mystic River winds through the southern portion of town, and Tufts University anchors the Medford Hillside neighborhood. Much of the city's housing stock dates from 1900 to 1940, a period when fieldstone rubble walls and brick foundations were the standard building practice — materials that have now endured more than a century of New England weather.

The soil conditions beneath Medford reflect its position along the Mystic River basin. Marine clay deposits from the post-glacial era underlie much of the city, particularly in South Medford and the Wellington area near the river. This clay expands when saturated and contracts during dry spells, creating cyclical lateral pressure against basement walls that slowly pushes them inward. The dense urban development means stormwater runoff concentrates quickly, and many of Medford's century-old foundation drainage systems — if they existed at all — have long since failed. The result is persistent moisture intrusion through deteriorated mortar joints and widening cracks in fieldstone and brick walls.
