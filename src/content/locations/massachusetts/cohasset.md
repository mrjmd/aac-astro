## Foundation Repair Services in Cohasset, Massachusetts

Cohasset is one of the South Shore's most picturesque coastal communities, where stately homes perch along a rocky Atlantic shoreline that has challenged builders for centuries. The town's housing stock spans from pre-Revolutionary fieldstone colonials to early twentieth-century estates and mid-century ranch homes, with foundation types ranging from hand-laid granite and fieldstone to poured concrete. Living this close to the ocean means foundations face year-round exposure to salt air, storm surge moisture, and the relentless freeze-thaw cycling that comes with a New England maritime climate.

Cohasset's underlying geology is defined by granite bedrock that often sits close to the surface, creating shallow soils with limited drainage capacity. When heavy rains or spring snowmelt saturate the thin glacial overburden, water has nowhere to go but laterally — directly against basement walls. Properties in lower-lying areas like Beechwood and near the harbor contend with water tables influenced by tidal fluctuations, while homes along Jerusalem Road and Sandy Beach face salt-laden groundwater that accelerates mortar deterioration in older stone foundations. Even poured concrete foundations from the mid-century building period show premature cracking where coastal conditions have intensified the normal aging process.
