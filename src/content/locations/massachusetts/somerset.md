## Foundation Repair Services in Somerset, Massachusetts

Somerset is a quiet residential town situated along the eastern bank of the Taunton River where it broadens into Mount Hope Bay. The town grew steadily during the post-war decades, and most of its housing stock dates from the 1950s through the 1970s — an era of poured concrete and concrete block foundations built on land that was often low-lying and close to the river's influence. After five to seven decades of service, these foundations are showing their age through cracking, water infiltration, and mortar joint deterioration that worsens with each passing season.

The Taunton River estuary creates uniquely challenging conditions for Somerset foundations. Tidal influence keeps the water table elevated across much of the town, particularly in neighborhoods like Riverside and Brayton Point that sit nearest the waterfront. The underlying soils are a mixture of glacial clay, silt, and river sediment that holds moisture tenaciously and drains poorly, maintaining constant hydrostatic pressure against basement walls. During spring thaw and heavy rain events, this pressure intensifies, forcing water through even hairline cracks in poured concrete and through deteriorating mortar joints in concrete block walls.
