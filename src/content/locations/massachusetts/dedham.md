## Foundation Repair Services in Dedham, Massachusetts

Dedham holds a distinctive place in Massachusetts history as both the county seat of Norfolk County and the home of Mother Brook, the oldest man-made canal in the United States. The town's residential character ranges from pre-war neighborhoods near Dedham Square with stately Colonials and Victorians to the post-war subdivisions of Oakdale and Greenlodge where Cape Cods and ranches line quiet streets. This mix of housing eras means Dedham contains an unusually wide variety of foundation types, from hand-laid fieldstone in the oldest sections to poured concrete and concrete block in the mid-century developments.

The geology beneath Dedham is shaped by the Neponset River to the south and Mother Brook cutting through the center of town. These waterways keep groundwater levels elevated across broad swaths of the community, particularly in the Riverdale and East Dedham lowlands. Glacial deposits in the area alternate between sandy outwash that drains quickly and pockets of compacted clay that hold water against foundation walls. Post-war homes built with minimal exterior waterproofing are especially susceptible after decades of freeze-thaw cycling have opened stress cracks in concrete and block walls.
