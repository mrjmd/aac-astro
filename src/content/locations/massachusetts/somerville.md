## Foundation Repair Services in Somerville, Massachusetts

Somerville is one of the most densely populated cities in New England, and its tightly packed streetscapes of triple-deckers and Victorian homes present unique foundation challenges. Built primarily between the 1890s and 1920s to house waves of immigrants working in nearby factories and brickyards, Somerville's housing stock sits on foundations that have endured well over a century of New England weather. Attack A Crack brings specialized expertise to every Somerville neighborhood, from the bustling streets of Davis Square to the rapidly developing Assembly Row waterfront.

The city's aggressive redevelopment—particularly around Union Square and Assembly Row—has raised the stakes for foundation integrity. As neighboring lots are excavated and developed, vibrations and changes in water flow patterns can stress existing foundations. Many Somerville homeowners discover new cracks or increased basement moisture following nearby construction projects. Our team understands these dynamics and provides repairs designed to withstand the ongoing changes in Somerville's urban landscape.

Somerville's older foundations frequently feature fieldstone or brick construction, materials that were standard in the late Victorian era but are now vulnerable to water infiltration and mortar deterioration. The city's clay-heavy soils retain moisture and expand during freeze-thaw cycles, placing repeated stress on aging foundation walls. Basements in Winter Hill, Ball Square, and the neighborhoods surrounding Tufts University often show telltale signs of water seepage along mortar joints and through hairline cracks in poured concrete sections.

Homeowners near the Assembly Row development and along the Mystic River corridor face additional concerns as Somerville's waterfront areas undergo transformation. Rising groundwater from altered drainage patterns and increased impervious surfaces in newly developed zones can redirect moisture toward existing foundations that were never designed for these conditions. Seasonal flooding along the Mystic further compounds the issue during spring snowmelt.
