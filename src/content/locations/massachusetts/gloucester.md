## Foundation Repair Services in Gloucester, Massachusetts

Gloucester is America's oldest seaport, and its residential architecture reflects centuries of building history stretching from colonial-era fishermen's cottages to Victorian-era sea captains' mansions and early twentieth-century bungalows. Most homes in town rest on fieldstone, granite, or brick foundations laid by hand long before modern concrete became standard. These historic foundations have endured generations of nor'easters, tidal surges, and the relentless salt air that defines life on Cape Ann, but time and moisture eventually take their toll on even the most solidly built stonework.

Cape Ann's geology is dominated by granite bedrock overlaid with thin glacial soils that drain quickly in some areas and trap water in others. Neighborhoods close to the harbor — Downtown Gloucester and East Gloucester — contend with high water tables influenced by tidal cycles, while hillside homes in Lanesville and Annisquam face runoff channeling that concentrates moisture against foundation walls during heavy rains. The salt air compounds these challenges by degrading lime-based mortar in fieldstone and granite foundations, causing joints to crumble and stones to shift over decades of exposure.

For newer poured concrete or brick foundations, our crack injection and drainage solutions stop water infiltration at the source.
