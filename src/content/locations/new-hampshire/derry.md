## Foundation Repair Services in Derry, New Hampshire

Derry, known as Spacetown for its connection to astronaut Alan Shepard, is one of southern New Hampshire's largest and fastest-growing communities. The town experienced massive residential development from the 1970s through the 1990s, resulting in neighborhood after neighborhood of single-family homes with poured concrete foundations. These foundations, now decades old, are increasingly showing the effects of New Hampshire's harsh climate. Homeowners in subdivisions throughout East Derry, Beaver Lake, and Kilrea Estates are discovering cracks that admit water during rainstorms and snowmelt.

Derry's geology plays a significant role in its foundation challenges. True to the Granite State name, bedrock often sits close to the surface, sometimes directly beneath a thin layer of topsoil. Builders during the development boom frequently encountered ledge, and homes built on partial ledge bear loads unevenly, with one section of foundation resting on immovable rock while another sits on compressible soil. Over decades, this differential creates stress that manifests as cracks, especially in poured concrete walls.
