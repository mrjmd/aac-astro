## Foundation Repair Services in Dover, New Hampshire

Dover, one of New Hampshire's oldest settlements and a key city in the Tri-Cities area, faces foundation challenges shaped by its river geography and centuries of development. Situated at the confluence of the Cochecho and Bellamy Rivers, the city's low-lying neighborhoods deal with elevated water tables that put constant hydrostatic pressure on basement walls. Homes near Downtown Dover and Dover Point are especially vulnerable during spring snowmelt, when rivers swell and groundwater rises to near-surface levels.

The city's housing spans a wide range of ages and construction styles. The historic core near the mill buildings features older homes with fieldstone and block foundations, while the suburban growth of the 1950s through 1970s produced neighborhoods of poured concrete basements in areas like Garrison Hill and Sawyers. New Hampshire's harsh freeze-thaw cycles affect all of these foundations, with frost penetrating 4-5 feet deep and cracking concrete walls that are already weakened by moisture exposure.
