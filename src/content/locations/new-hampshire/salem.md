## Foundation Repair Services in Salem, New Hampshire

Salem sits right on the Massachusetts border, a suburban community that grew rapidly during the 1970s, 1980s, and 1990s as families moved north seeking New Hampshire's lower tax burden. This growth period means much of Salem's housing stock features poured concrete foundations that are now 30 to 50 years old, an age when stress cracks and water infiltration commonly appear. The town's landscape varies from low-lying areas near Canobie Lake and Arlington Pond to rocky, ledge-heavy terrain in the northern sections, creating diverse foundation conditions across relatively short distances.

New Hampshire's deep frost line is a major factor in Salem's foundation problems. With frost penetrating 4-5 feet into the ground each winter, the full depth of most basement walls endures cyclical freezing and thawing. Soil moisture expands as it freezes, then contracts as it thaws, placing repetitive stress on concrete that eventually opens cracks. In areas where bedrock sits close to the surface, homes built on ledge can experience different settlement patterns than those on deeper soils, sometimes leading to diagonal stress cracks.
