## Foundation Repair Services in Londonderry, New Hampshire

Londonderry is one of southern New Hampshire's most desirable residential communities, a town that transformed from apple orchards and farmland into thriving suburban neighborhoods over the past four decades. Much of Londonderry's housing stock dates from the 1980s through the 2000s, making it newer than many neighboring towns. However, even these relatively modern foundations are not immune to New Hampshire's climate. Poured concrete walls in subdivisions throughout town are developing cracks as they age through decades of freeze-thaw cycles.

The town's terrain is classic southern New Hampshire: a mix of glacial deposits, clay soils, and granite ledge with minimal topsoil cover. In many Londonderry neighborhoods, builders had to blast or excavate ledge to create basement space, leaving foundations that bear on a patchwork of rock and soil. This uneven bearing surface can lead to differential settling over time. Additionally, Londonderry's position between the Merrimack River valley and the upland terrain to the east creates variable groundwater conditions that change with the seasons.
