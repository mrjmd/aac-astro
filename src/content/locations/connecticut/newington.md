## Foundation Repair Services in Newington, Connecticut

Newington is a classic Greater Hartford suburb that grew rapidly during the post-war building boom. The town's housing stock is predominantly mid-century—cape cods, ranches, and colonials built from the 1950s through the 1970s on poured concrete foundations. While these homes were well-built for their era, decades of New England weather have taken their toll on their foundations.

The soil composition in Newington mirrors much of central Connecticut: a mix of glacial till and clay that expands with moisture and contracts during dry periods. This cyclical movement puts lateral pressure on foundation walls, and over 50-70 years, cracks inevitably develop. Most are vertical shrinkage cracks that allow water into basements during heavy rain—a nuisance that becomes a serious problem if left unaddressed.
