## Foundation Repair Services in Ledyard, Connecticut

Ledyard is a southeastern Connecticut town shaped by its proximity to the Thames River and the Naval Submarine Base in neighboring Groton. The town grew rapidly during the Cold War era as military families and defense workers settled in new subdivisions across Gales Ferry, Bill Hill, and Ledyard Center. This building boom produced thousands of homes with poured concrete foundations now reaching 40 to 60 years of age.

The Thames River valley geology beneath Ledyard presents specific foundation challenges. Marine clay deposits from post-glacial flooding underlie much of the western part of town, particularly the Gales Ferry corridor. These clays are highly reactive—swelling with moisture during wet seasons and contracting during dry periods—creating cyclical lateral pressure on basement walls. Homes closer to the Thames face elevated water tables that compound the problem, while homes on higher ground toward Ledyard Shores contend with glacial till that channels runoff unpredictably.
