## Foundation Repair Services in Windham, Connecticut

Windham—home to the borough of Willimantic, known as "Thread City" for its textile mill heritage—is one of eastern Connecticut's most historically significant communities. The town's housing stock reflects that history: triple-deckers and worker cottages from the 1890s through 1940s line the streets of Willimantic, while mid-century ranch homes and colonials fill the outer neighborhoods of North Windham, South Windham, and Windham Heights.

The confluence of the Willimantic River and the Natchaug River defines Windham's geology and its foundation challenges. River-deposited alluvial clays blanket the valley floor, particularly through the Willimantic borough. These clay soils hold moisture tenaciously, creating sustained hydrostatic pressure against basement walls during wet months and shrinking away during droughts to allow settlement. The older mill-era homes face compounded risk—their fieldstone and brick foundations were built before modern waterproofing techniques, and over a century of freeze-thaw cycling has taken a measurable toll.
