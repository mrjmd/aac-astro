## Foundation Repair Services in New Britain, Connecticut

Known as the "Hardware City" for its manufacturing heritage, New Britain features block after block of worker housing built during the city's industrial peak in the early-to-mid 20th century. These well-built homes were designed to last, but their foundations are now facing the inevitable effects of time, soil movement, and Connecticut's demanding climate.

New Britain sits on glacial deposits that include clay-heavy soils, particularly in the lower elevations near Stanley Park and the downtown area. These soils retain moisture and swell during wet periods, then shrink during dry spells—creating a constant push-and-pull against foundation walls. Homes in Shuttle Meadow and Pinnacle Heights, built on slightly higher ground, often face different challenges with ledge and drainage patterns.
