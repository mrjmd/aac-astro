## Foundation Repair Services in Middletown, Connecticut

Home to Wesleyan University and situated along a sweeping bend of the Connecticut River, Middletown features an architectural diversity that spans three centuries. From the historic homes along Main Street and High Street to the mid-century developments in South Farms and Westlake, Middletown's housing stock reflects the city's long and varied history.

The Connecticut River's influence on Middletown's geology is profound. River valley soils—a mix of sand, silt, and clay deposited over millennia—create variable conditions that affect foundations differently depending on elevation and location. Lower-lying areas near the river and in Maromas deal with higher water tables, while the Highland neighborhood and upper sections of town sit on more stable glacial deposits but still face New England's relentless freeze-thaw cycles.
