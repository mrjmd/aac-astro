## Foundation Repair Services in Cranston, Rhode Island

Cranston is Rhode Island's third-largest city and borders Providence to the south, spanning a landscape that ranges from the urban neighborhoods of Knightsville and Edgewood to the more rural stretches of Western Cranston. Much of the city's housing stock was built during the post-war building boom of the 1940s through 1960s, featuring poured concrete and concrete block foundations that are now showing their age after decades of exposure to New England's demanding climate.

The Pawtuxet River runs through Cranston, and its floodplain influences groundwater levels across a wide swath of the city. Glacial deposits left behind clay-heavy soils that retain moisture and expand when saturated, placing lateral pressure on basement walls. Homeowners in Edgewood and along the Narragansett Bay shoreline contend with additional moisture from coastal weather patterns, while properties in Western Cranston encounter different challenges from the rocky, uneven terrain of the interior uplands.
